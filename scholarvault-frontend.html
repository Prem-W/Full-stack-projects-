📬 How to Contact a Professor — The Complete Guide

1
Research before you write
Read 2–3 of their recent papers. Know their current project. Mention a specific paper by exact title. Professors receive 50–100 cold emails per week — the ones that get a reply prove the student did real homework.
2
Write a specific, informative subject line
Use: Prospective [Degree] Student — [Research Topic] — [Your Institution]
✓ Good
  • "Prospective PhD — Federated Learning — IIT Bombay"
  • "Research Inquiry — CRISPR off-target — NUS"
  • "MS Applicant — Transformer efficiency — KAIST"
✗ Bad
  • "Seeking PhD admission"
  • "I want to work with you"
  • "Respected Sir — Study Abroad"
  • "Application 2025"
3
Email structure — maximum 250 words
Subject: Prospective PhD — [Specific Research Topic] — [Your University]
Para 1 — Who you are: "I am [Name], final-year [Degree] at [University, Country], applying for [program] starting [Year]."

Para 2 — Paper mention: "I read your paper '[Exact Title]' ([Year]). Your approach to [specific method] was insightful because [one concrete reason]."

Para 3 — Your credentials: "GPA: [X/Y]. Key project: [brief]. Publications: [if any]."

Para 4 — Ask: "Are you accepting PhD students for [Year]? I'd welcome any guidance on your process."

Close: "I've attached my CV and 1-page research statement. Thank you."
4
Timing, follow-up & etiquette
• Best time: Tue–Thu, 9–11am in the professor's timezone.
• Fall intake: email Aug–Oct. Spring intake: email Jun–Aug.
• Wait 12–14 days then follow up once only.
• Follow-up: "Following up on my email from [date]. I remain very interested in your work on [topic]."
• No reply after 2 attempts — move on. It's not personal.
5
Absolute rules
✗ Never
  • Same email to 50+ professors
  • Ask for admission decision in first email
  • Attach full application (SOP, transcripts, LORs)
  • "Respected Sir/Madam" — use their name
  • Email on weekends / public holidays
  • More than 250 words total
✓ Always
  • "Dear Prof. [Last Name],"
  • Mention a specific recent paper by exact title
  • State GPA, test scores, publications upfront
  • Attach only CV (1 page) + research statement
  • Use university email if possible
  • Include Google Scholar / GitHub links
